Brianna Milot

Undergraduate researcher and aspiring botanist

Summa Cum Laude Graduate from the University of Utah

▼

My education, my research, and my life revolves around plants. My interest in plants began as a child reading plant guide books and visiting botanical gardens. As I grew up, I became increasingly curious about the world around me and our impact as humans. My degree in college focused on environmental policy, statistical analysis, and social movements for environmental change. I found myself continuously drawn towards plants, pursuing two separate undergraduate research projects that studied an invasive plant species and the influence of climatic legacy effects on local Cottonwood trees.


These research projects inspired and fueled my desire to search for similar opportunities to investigate and better understand our changing world. My research interests focus on the responses of plants and their ecological communities to environmental change. I intend to pursue a graduate degree in Biology in order to further understand the dynamic between humans and plants.

▼

With over four semesters of paid undergraduate research, two presentations, and one publication, I sought as many research opportunities as I possibly could throughout my undergraduate degree. I worked in three different labs, with my work ranging from slicing sediment cores, preparing slides of charcoal and pollen data, processing soil samples for acid digestion, and testing soil characteristics. I also worked on two research projects that involved field work, including conducting vegetation surveys and collecting soil cores.


These experiences were invaluable opportunities that exposed me to common lab procedures, important lab protocols, and familiarity with scientific processes. I also had the opportunity to present two research projects at research symposiums, enabling me to share my research with other scientists and the community. Additionally, my research has been published by the Undergraduate Research Journal at the University of Utah and mentioned in a local newspaper. Through these experiences, I found my passion and began my research journey. I plan on continuing this path of exploration and discovery with a PhD in Biology.
